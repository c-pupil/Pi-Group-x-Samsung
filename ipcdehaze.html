Abstract

Image dehazing for Industrial PC (IPC) cameras poses unique challenges due to limited computational resources and the need for high-resolution, real-time processing. Existing deep learning methods often suffer from high latency or fail to generalize well across different atmospheric conditions.

We present IPCDehaze, a lightweight, physics-inspired network designed specifically for edge devices. By incorporating the atmospheric scattering model into the network architecture, we achieve a balance between physical interpretability and learning capability. Our method runs in real-time on mobile chips while delivering state-of-the-art dehazing performance, significantly improving visibility for downstream surveillance and monitoring tasks.
